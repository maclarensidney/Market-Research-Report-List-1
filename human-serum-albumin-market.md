<p><p>The Human Serum Albumin (HSA) market is segmented into two primary types: recombinant and plasma-derived. Recombinant HSA is produced through genetically engineered microorganisms, ensuring consistent quality and reduced risk of contamination. Conversely, plasma-derived HSA is extracted from human blood plasma, offering a traditional source but with variability based on donor populations. Both types serve critical roles in medical applications, including fluid management, drug delivery, and treating conditions like hypoalbuminemia, highlighting their significance in healthcare.</p></p>
