<p><p>The aviation kerosene market primarily consists of three types: Jet A, Jet A-1, and Jet B. Jet A is commonly used in the United States, characterized by its higher freezing point. Jet A-1, widely accepted internationally, offers a lower freezing point, making it suitable for long-haul flights in colder climates. Jet B, a more thermally efficient option, contains a mixture of kerosene and gasoline, used mainly in very cold regions. Each type caters to specific operational needs in aviation.</p></p>
