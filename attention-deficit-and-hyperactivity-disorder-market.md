<p><p>The Attention Deficit and Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) market is primarily divided into two categories: stimulant and non-stimulant treatments. Stimulant medications, such as amphetamines and methylphenidate, are the most commonly prescribed and work by enhancing the levels of neurotransmitters in the brain, improving focus and reducing hyperactive behaviors. Non-stimulant medications, like atomoxetine, offer an alternative for patients who may experience side effects from stimulants or have specific health concerns. Both categories aim to manage ADHD symptoms but differ in their mechanisms and patient responses.</p></p>
