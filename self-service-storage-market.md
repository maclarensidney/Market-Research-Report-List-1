<p><p>The self-service storage market comprises two main types: climate-controlled and non-climate-controlled storage. Climate-controlled self-storage units maintain regulated temperature and humidity, protecting sensitive items like electronics, artwork, and documents from environmental damage. In contrast, non-climate-controlled self-storage offers basic shelter without temperature regulation, suitable for robust items like furniture or tools. While climate-controlled facilities typically have higher rental costs, they cater to customers needing extra protection, whereas non-climate options attract those seeking affordability and convenience.</p></p>
