<p><p>The Pelvis Internal Fixation System market comprises two main types: locking and non-locking systems. Locking systems utilize locking screws and plates, providing enhanced stability and reduced risk of screw backout, making them suitable for complex fractures. Non-locking systems rely on traditional screw-and-plate mechanisms, offering simpler installation and flexibility in some cases. Both types serve specific clinical needs, with locking systems generally preferred for their biomechanical advantages, while non-locking systems may appeal to those seeking less invasive options.</p></p>
