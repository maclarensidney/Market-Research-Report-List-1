<p><p>Residential HVAC equipment includes various systems designed to regulate indoor climate. Direct Expansion Systems feature individual units like ductless mini-split systems, allowing for localized cooling and heating, enhancing energy efficiency. In contrast, Central Air Conditioning Systems are designed to cool and distribute air throughout an entire home via ducts, providing uniform temperature control. Both systems cater to different needs, with direct expansion systems ideal for specific zones and central systems favored for comprehensive home comfort and convenience.</p></p>
