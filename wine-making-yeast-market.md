<p><p>The wine-making yeast market primarily features two types: yeast extract and autolyzed yeast. Yeast extract is derived from the breakdown of yeast cells, enriching wines with nutrients, flavors, and enhancing fermentation processes. It promotes a more complex flavor profile in wines. Autolyzed yeast, a product of fully broken-down yeast, releases essential compounds that contribute to umami and mouthfeel, improving the overall sensory experience of the wine. Both types play crucial roles in wine production, influencing taste, quality, and stability.</p></p>
