<p><p>The sheet metal stamping part market encompasses various processes that manipulate metal into desired shapes. Blanking involves cutting out flat shapes from sheets, while embossing creates raised designs. Bending shapes metal by applying force, whereas coining uses high pressure to form detailed impressions. Flanging involves bending edges to strengthen components or facilitate assembly. Additionally, other methods may include deep drawing and perforating, catering to diverse industrial applications in automotive, electronics, and consumer goods sectors.</p></p>
