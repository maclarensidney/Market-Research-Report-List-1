<p><p>Acrylonitrile (ACN) is a crucial chemical used in producing synthetic fibers, plastics, and resins. The market for ACN is primarily segmented into two production methods: the Propylene Method and the Propane Method. The Propylene Method involves the reaction of propylene with ammonia and oxygen to yield ACN, while the Propane Method uses propane as a feedstock, undergoing dehydrogenation to produce acrylic compounds that are subsequently converted to ACN. Both methods influence production costs and environmental impact in the ACN market.</p></p>
